## WALLET MANAGEMENT

### What is a wallet?
A wallet in Fin-Dinari represents your money account. You can have multiple wallets in different countries and currencies. Each wallet has:
- A country (where the wallet is based)
- A balance (your money)
- A currency (DT, EUR, USD, GBP, etc.)

### How to create a wallet?
To create a wallet:
1. Go to "Services" in the navigation menu
2. Click on "Budget Management"
3. Click the "Create Wallet" button
4. Fill in the country name
5. Enter the initial balance
6. Select the currency (DT, EUR, USD, GBP, etc.)
7. Click "Create Wallet"

Your wallet will be created and visible on the wallets page.

### How to view my wallets?
You can view all your wallets by:
1. Going to "Services" → "Budget Management"
2. All your wallets will be displayed as cards
3. Each card shows country, balance, and currency

### How to search for a wallet?
On the wallets page, you can:
- Use the search bar to search by country or currency
- Sort by country, balance, or currency

### How to edit a wallet?
To edit a wallet:
1. Go to "Services" → "Budget Management"
2. Find the wallet you want to edit
3. Click the "Edit" button on the wallet card
4. Modify the country, balance, or currency
5. Click "Update Wallet"

### How to delete a wallet?
To delete a wallet:
1. Go to "Services" → "Budget Management"
2. Find the wallet you want to delete
3. Click the "Delete" button (red trash icon)
4. Confirm the deletion

⚠️ **Warning:** Deleting a wallet will also delete all investments linked to it!

### Can I have wallets in different currencies?
Yes! You can create wallets in multiple currencies:
- DT (Tunisian Dinar)
- EUR (Euro)
- USD (US Dollar)
- GBP (British Pound)
- And any other currency you need

### How do wallets work with investments?
When you make an investment, you select which wallet to use. The invested amount is taken from that wallet's balance. When the investment matures, the returns are credited back to the same wallet.

## INVESTMENT PROFIT CALCULATOR

### How does the profit calculator work?
When creating or editing an investment, you'll see a live calculator that shows:
- Interest rate of the selected obligation
- Duration in months
- Amount you're investing
- Expected profit (calculated as: Amount × Interest Rate ÷ 100)
- Total return (Amount + Profit)

The calculator updates in real-time as you type the amount!

### Example calculation:
If you invest 10,000 DT in an obligation with 8% interest:
- Profit = 10,000 × (8 ÷ 100) = 800 DT
- Total Return = 10,000 + 800 = 10,800 DT

### How is maturity date calculated?
Maturity Date = Purchase Date + Duration (in months)

Example: If you invest on 15/04/2025 for 12 months, maturity date is 15/04/2026.

## PDF CONTRACT FEATURES

### How to download an investment contract as PDF?
To download a PDF contract for your investment:
1. Go to "My Investments" page
2. Click on "View" on any investment
3. Scroll down to find the "DOWNLOAD CONTRACT" button
4. Click the button
5. The PDF will be downloaded automatically

### What does the PDF contract include?
The PDF contract includes:
- Invoice number and date
- Loan type (Short/Medium/Long term)
- Interest rate
- Invested amount
- Start date and end date
- Duration in days
- Invested amount summary
- Calculated interest
- Total to repay
- Lender signature section
- Borrower signature section
- Terms and conditions
- Company information

### How to upload a PDF to create an investment?
To import an investment from a PDF contract:
1. Go to "My Investments" page
2. Click the "Upload PDF" button
3. Drag and drop your PDF file or click to browse
4. Select the PDF file (max 5MB)
5. Click "Extract & Preview"
6. The system will automatically extract:
   - Investment amount
   - Obligation name
   - Interest rate (if available)
   - Purchase date
   - Maturity date
7. Review the extracted information
8. Select your wallet
9. Select the matching obligation
10. Click "Confirm & Create Investment"

### What PDF formats are supported?
Supported formats: Investment contracts, loan agreements, bank statements (must have readable text, not scanned images).

## PDF UPLOAD & EXTRACTION

### How does PDF extraction work?
When you upload a PDF, the system:
1. Reads the text from the PDF
2. Searches for patterns to find:
   - Amount (numbers followed by DT, TND, Dinar)
   - Obligation name (after "Obligation:", "Loan Type:", etc.)
   - Interest rate (numbers followed by %)
   - Dates (dd/mm/yyyy format)
   - Duration (numbers followed by "months")
3. Shows you a preview of what was found
4. Asks you to confirm before creating the investment

### What if the PDF doesn't extract correctly?
If extraction fails, you can:
- Manually enter the investment details
- Check that your PDF has readable text (not scanned)
- Ensure the PDF contains the required information (amount and obligation name)

### What information is required for successful extraction?
Minimum required:
- Investment amount (e.g., "10,000 DT")
- Obligation name (e.g., "Safe Bond")

Nice to have (will be auto-filled if found):
- Interest rate
- Purchase date
- Maturity date
- Duration

## TROUBLESHOOTING

### Common Issues and Solutions

**Issue: The profit calculator doesn't update**
Solution: Make sure you've selected an obligation first. The calculator only works after an obligation is selected.

**Issue: PDF upload fails**
Solution: Ensure your PDF is less than 5MB and contains readable text (not a scanned image).

**Issue: Notifications not showing**
Solution: Refresh the page. Notifications are checked every 30 seconds automatically.

**Issue: Can't find the upload PDF button**
Solution: Go to "My Investments" page. The button is next to "Browse Obligations".

**Issue: Maturity alert not showing**
Solution: Make sure you have investments maturing within 7 days. Alerts only show on homepage and investments page.

**Issue: Crypto prices not loading**
Solution: Check your internet connection. Prices refresh every 60 seconds.

**Issue: Financial news not loading**
Solution: The API key may need to be refreshed. Try again later.
